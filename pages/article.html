ADAS: Advanced Driver Assistance Systems OR Automated Data Acquisition Systems?

I recently happened to read a fantastic book titled “The driver in the driverless car” by Vivek Wadhwa. The book tells you how we, as someone born in the 80s perceive today’s trends in automating things and compare our learning techniques to that of today’s technological advancements. But, in parallel, this book also made me think about the current technological (so-called) challenges.

Nowadays, the growing demand for ADAS has taken the automotive engineering research to a new level. Various ADAS features which were thought some years ago of being just a dream are today working seamlessly to address one of the major concerns of driver safety. Almost all of the top-tier OEMs are seen pitching a fierce competition regarding the actual implementation of these features and all of them have their own strategy in making these systems work.

But the fundamental logic behind application of these features remains the same considering the different vehicle environments/scenarios. It was after all, the driver’s acumen that enabled the quest for driving any vehicle through any precarious situation. Today’s ADAS focuses on assisting the driver in addressing some of the key areas that can alleviate fatigue and can allow him to drive with elevated speeds and improved confidence. ADAS also assists during specific everyday scenarios such as parking, blind spot detection, rain-sensing wiping, etc.

On the other hand, L5 completely eliminates the driver (human) from the car! To me this seems scary, and I feel this can ONLY be possible in an environment where road manners are followed to the full-extent and road discipline is maintained by all the users around the vehicle (be it pedestrians, cyclists, other cars, parked cars, etc.).

What feels initially as a benign thought is actually a despicable threat in coming years… The looming danger which nobody wishes to talk about (again with rise of AI…!) is, aren’t we (as humans) indirectly creating a clone (against an actual driver) that can operate by gradually learning how the actual driver normally drives (by making use of ML algorithms..)? Aren’t we knowingly (or unknowingly) feeding this DATA (in form of driver-initiated actions) to various intelligent systems within a car?

The primary danger here is HOW can the automakers standardize the driver profile (as driver behaviors change drastically)? Imagine you sitting in your car in a fit of rage (maybe due to some altercation or due to some stress-related fall-out) and the car refuses to start! I can visualize and appreciate the safety aspect of this scenario but aren’t we subconsciously creating a humanoid?

The data acquisition is not just limited to what you feed in the algorithm but also HOW is the input received (through sensors). Varying amounts of fist pressure, for instance, on the steering wheel may be an input for an advanced driving profile.

What intrigues me is the fact that everybody knows by now what the smartphone companies have done with our data and how is our data smartly curated/processed and fed back to us! But, what may be acceptable in case of a smartphone may not be acceptable for a driving scenario!
